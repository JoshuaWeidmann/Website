Working Papers

  1. "Thanks for Sharing Your Story: Using AI Chatbots to Conduct Qualitative Interviews in Mass Surveys" (with Michael M. Bechtel, Aaron Cannon, and Michael Hess). [Abstract]

    The availability of artificial intelligence (AI) enables researchers to qualitatively study preference formation and individual behavior requiring unstructured question formats that used to be too costly for inclusion in mass surveys. We experimentally assess the performance of an AI chatbot to elicit preferences, personal experiences, and beliefs through qualitative interviews against the more traditional survey-approach of open-ended questions. We field multiple surveys to convenience and quota samples of the adult populations in the US and Germany to evaluate AI-chatbot performance with respect to informational value, perceived survey quality, price per complete, subjective rapport, and emotional state. Our applications include political science (vote choice) and consumer research (drivers of purchasing behavior) questions. Since qualitative interviews may increase the willingness to provide truthful responses to sensitive questions that are prone to social desirability bias, we also investigate attitudes toward abortion, weight loss, and domestic violence. The results offer guidance to researchers and practitioners interested in a more qualitative assessment of the structure of mass preferences through the use of AI technology.
